# AGRICULTURE IN INDIA: CONCISE NOTES

## Introduction
- 2/3 of population engaged in agriculture
- Produces food and industrial raw materials
- Export crops: tea, coffee, spices
- Primary economic activity since ancient times

## Farming Systems

| System | Characteristics | Regional Distribution |
|--------|----------------|------------------------|
| **Primitive Subsistence** | • Uses primitive tools (hoe, dao, digging sticks)<br>• "Slash and burn" technique<br>• Monsoon-dependent<br>• Low productivity<br>• Allows natural soil fertility replenishment | • Jhumming (Northeast India)<br>• Pamlou (Manipur)<br>• Dipa (Bastar, A&N Islands)<br>• Bewar/Dahiya (MP)<br>• Podu/Penda (AP)<br>• Pama Dabi/Koman/Bringa (Odisha)<br>• Kumari (W. Ghats)<br>• Valre/Waltre (SE Rajasthan)<br>• Khil (Himalayas)<br>• Kuruwa (Jharkhand) |
| **Intensive Subsistence** | • Labor-intensive<br>• High biochemical inputs<br>• Irrigation used<br>• Small, often uneconomical landholdings<br>• High population pressure areas<br>• Maximum output from limited land | • Areas with high population density<br>• Fragmented landholdings due to inheritance laws |
| **Commercial** | • Heavy use of modern inputs (HYV seeds, fertilizers, pesticides)<br>• Focus on higher productivity<br>• Varying commercialization by region<br>• Same crop may be commercial in one region, subsistence in another | • Punjab, Haryana (rice as commercial)<br>• Odisha (rice as subsistence) |
| **Plantation** | • Single-crop cultivation on large areas<br>• Integrated agro-industrial processes<br>• Capital-intensive<br>• Uses migrant labor<br>• Well-developed transportation<br>• Interface of agriculture and industry | • Tea (Assam, N. Bengal)<br>• Coffee (Karnataka, Kerala, TN)<br>• Rubber (Kerala, TN)<br>• Sugarcane<br>• Banana |

## Cropping Seasons

| Season | Timing | Major Crops | Characteristics | Major Regions |
|--------|--------|-------------|----------------|---------------|
| **Rabi** | • Sowing: Oct-Dec<br>• Harvest: Apr-Jun | • Wheat<br>• Barley<br>• Peas<br>• Gram<br>• Mustard | • Needs cool growing season<br>• Benefits from western temperate cyclones<br>• Enhanced by Green Revolution<br>• Requires bright sunshine during ripening | • Punjab<br>• Haryana<br>• HP<br>• J&K<br>• Uttarakhand<br>• UP<br>• W. Rajasthan |
| **Kharif** | • Sowing: With monsoon onset<br>• Harvest: Sep-Oct | • Paddy<br>• Maize<br>• Jowar<br>• Bajra<br>• Tur, Moong, Urad<br>• Cotton<br>• Jute<br>• Groundnut<br>• Soyabean | • Heavily dependent on timing/quantity of rainfall<br>• Forms traditional Indian agriculture<br>• Triple paddy in some states | • Throughout India according to crop requirements |
| **Zaid** | • Between rabi and kharif | • Watermelon<br>• Muskmelon<br>• Cucumber<br>• Vegetables<br>• Fodder | • Short growing season<br>• Quick-growing crops<br>• Usually requires irrigation due to summer months | • Irrigated areas |

## Major Crops

### Food Grains

| Crop | Climate Requirements | Characteristics | Major Producers |
|------|----------------------|-----------------|-----------------|
| **Rice** | • >25°C temperature<br>• High humidity<br>• >100cm rainfall<br>• Can grow with irrigation in low rainfall areas | • 2nd largest producer (after China)<br>• Kharif crop<br>• Plains, coastal, delta regions<br>• Three crops in some regions: Aus, Aman, Boro<br>• Staple food of majority in India | • Assam<br>• WB<br>• Odisha<br>• AP, Telangana<br>• TN<br>• Kerala<br>• Maharashtra<br>• UP<br>• Bihar<br>• Punjab<br>• Haryana |
| **Wheat** | • Cool growing season<br>• 50-75cm rainfall<br>• Bright sunshine during ripening | • 2nd most important cereal<br>• Rabi crop<br>• Two major zones: Ganga-Satluj plains, Deccan black soil | • Punjab<br>• Haryana<br>• UP<br>• MP<br>• Bihar<br>• Rajasthan |
| **Millets** | • Jowar: Rain-fed<br>• Bajra: Thrives in sandy/shallow black soils<br>• Ragi: Grows in dry regions | • High nutritional value<br>• Jowar: 3rd most important food crop<br>• Ragi: Rich in iron, calcium, micro nutrients | • Jowar: Maharashtra, Karnataka, AP, MP<br>• Bajra: Rajasthan, UP, Maharashtra, Gujarat<br>• Ragi: Karnataka, TN, Himalayan states, Jharkhand |
| **Maize** | • 21-27°C temperature<br>• Grows well in old alluvial soil | • Food and fodder<br>• Kharif crop generally<br>• Rabi in Bihar<br>• Modern inputs increasing yields | • Karnataka<br>• MP<br>• UP<br>• Bihar<br>• AP<br>• Telangana |
| **Pulses** | • Need less moisture<br>• Survive in dry conditions | • India leads production/consumption globally<br>• Major protein source<br>• Types: tur, urad, moong, masur, peas, gram<br>• Nitrogen-fixing (except arhar)<br>• Drought-resistant<br>• Rotational crops | • MP<br>• Rajasthan<br>• Maharashtra<br>• UP<br>• Karnataka |

### Horticultural Crops
- 2nd globally after China (2020) in fruits and vegetables production
- Both tropical and temperate fruits grown in India
- Regional specialties:
  - Mangoes: Maharashtra, AP, Telangana, UP, WB
  - Oranges: Nagpur, Cherrapunjee (Meghalaya)
  - Bananas: Kerala, Mizoram, Maharashtra, TN
  - Lichi/guava: UP, Bihar
  - Pineapples: Meghalaya
  - Grapes: AP, Telangana, Maharashtra
  - Apples, pears, apricots, walnuts: J&K, HP
- Key vegetables: pea, cauliflower, onion, cabbage, tomato, brinjal, potato
- Requires specialized conditions and infrastructure

## Technological and Institutional Reforms

**Land Reforms**
- Collectivization and consolidation of holdings
- Abolition of zamindari system
- Bhoodan-Gramdan movement by Vinoba Bhave ("Blood-less Revolution")
  - Voluntary land donation to landless farmers
- Main focus of First Five Year Plan; laws enacted but implementation often weak

**Agricultural Revolutions**
- Green Revolution (package technology) - improved seed varieties, fertilizers
- White Revolution (Operation Flood) - cooperative dairy network
- Initially concentrated development in selected areas

**Comprehensive Development**
- 1980s-90s initiatives combining institutional and technical reforms
- Focus on sustainable land use practices

**Support Systems**
- Financial: 
  - Crop insurance against drought, flood, cyclone, fire, disease
  - Grameen banks/cooperatives
  - Low-interest loans
  - Kisan Credit Card (KCC)
  - Personal Accident Insurance Scheme (PAIS)
- Price:
  - Minimum support price
  - Remunerative/procurement prices
  - Protection from speculators and middlemen
- Information:
  - Weather bulletins for farmers
  - Agricultural radio/TV programs
  - The Farmers' Portal
